WESAT - Women Engineered Satellite

WESAT (Women Engineered SATellite) is a student-developed payload created at the Lal Bahadur Shastri Institute of Technology for Women (LBSITW), led and executed by an all-women team. Its primary mission is to directly measure ultraviolet (UV) radiation and solar irradiance in space and compare those measurements with ground readings from a college ground-station in Thiruvananthapuram. WESAT was launched as a rideshare payload on ISROâ€™s PSLV-C58 mission on 1 January 2024.

Science Goals â€” Why measure UV from a tiny satellite?

Produce point-source (direct) measurements of UV intensity and solar irradiance above the atmosphere, rather than relying on multispectral/spectroradiometer-derived maps. This helps estimate how much UV is filtered by the atmosphere and can inform climate studies and UV exposure alerts for Kerala.
Compare space measurements with simultaneous ground measurements (from their campus ground station) to compute filtering rates and better understand local atmospheric effects (ozone, aerosols, clouds).

Key Technical Highlights

Instruments: the payload carries UV sensors (UVA band focus) and solar irradiance sensors (pyranometer-type). The sensors output small voltages (mV) that are converted to power (W/mÂ²) and used to compute a UV Index (UVI) via a conversion formula the team gives in its technical report.

Self-powered measurement concept: the WESAT payload is designed so its measurement elements generate the electrical signal directly from incident sunlight (via photovoltaic effect + optical filtering). Because of this, the payload did not require powering up from a battery once deployed â€” it began reporting telemetry as soon as it was exposed to sunlight during ascent (even before reaching the final POEM orbit). That is a claimed distinctive feature.
Dynamic range: reported instrument dynamic range supports up to ~2000 W/mÂ² for solar irradiance and ~120 W/mÂ² for UV (giving a computed UVI range up to ~31). The report gives practical telemetry examples (space UVI readings far exceed simultaneous ground UVI because the atmosphere attenuates radiation).

Why WESAT Matters

Science & Policy: provides localized UV/solar irradiance datasets for Kerala.
Education: a hands-on student satellite project fostering strong STEM skills.
Representation: a women-led payload design milestone, symbolizing gender equity in space.
